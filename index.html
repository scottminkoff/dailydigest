OTHER THINGS TO NOTE

The stoker for a stapler is actually called an "anvil," and many models feature a rotating plate that changes the staple direction from inward (for permanent fastening) to outward (for temporary pinning). While a staple of office design since the mid-20th century, the "pinning" function is rarely used by modern workers. This mechanical quirk remains a standard feature despite its obsolescence in daily use.
