Additionally, the chambers propose adjusting the Foundation Aid formula for vulnerable student populations and advancing various affordable housing measures that deviate from executive priorities.

New York City Government and Politics

New York City Comptroller Mark Levine has issued a sobering assessment of Mayor Zohran Mamdani’s $127 billion preliminary budget, projecting a massive structural deficit. Levine's office calculates a gap of at least $3.6 billion due to overly optimistic Wall Street tax revenue projections, which expands to over $7 billion when discounting Mamdani's politically unviable property tax hikes. The Comptroller also criticized the administration's reliance on "rainy day" reserves to balance the budget during a fundamentally strong national economy. In response to the looming fiscal crisis, Levine expressed a new willingness to support tax increases on millionaires and major corporations to help close the shortfall.
