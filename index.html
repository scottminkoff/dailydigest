US News and Politics

The political alliance between President Trump and Rep. Marjorie Taylor Greene has fractured, with Trump calling her a “ranting lunatic” on Truth Social and signaling his support for a primary challenge against her. The break followed Greene’s recent calls for the release of the Justice Department’s files on Jeffrey Epstein. In response, Greene posted screenshots of her texts to Trump and criticized his attack.

House Republican leaders plan to hold a vote on Tuesday on legislation that would force the full release of the Justice Department's records on Jeffrey Epstein. Attorney General Pam Bondi has appointed Jay Clayton, the U.S. attorney in Manhattan, to lead a new investigation into Epstein after Trump demanded a probe into prominent Democrats. Newly released documents show Epstein exchanged text messages with Del. Stacey Plaskett of the U.S. Virgin Islands during a congressional hearing on Michael Cohen.

The Trump administration has transformed the Department of Homeland Security to carry out its mass deportation campaign, a shift that has reportedly diverted resources from efforts to combat child predators and sex trafficking. Border Patrol agents began an immigration crackdown in Charlotte, N.C., over the weekend. According to The Dispatch, the agency is also using nativist messaging in its recruitment efforts, framing its mission as preserving American culture.

Internal turmoil has erupted at the Heritage Foundation following President Kevin Roberts' defense of Tucker Carlson's recent friendly interview with white nationalist Nick Fuentes. According to The Dispatch, Roberts’ refusal to condemn Carlson sparked outrage among some Heritage staffers and has highlighted a broader division on the right over antisemitic elements within the conservative movement. Sen. Lindsey Graham stated, "I’m in the ‘Hitler sucks’ wing of the Republican Party."

In economic news, President Trump issued an executive order rolling back his "reciprocal" tariffs on certain agricultural imports like beef, tomatoes, and coffee, a move that comes amid political pressure over high grocery prices. The delayed September jobs report is now scheduled for release this Thursday. Meanwhile, President Trump also announced he was killing the U.S. penny. In other news, a Federal Reserve governor who stepped down in August was found to have repeatedly violated the central bank’s stock trading rules.

With the government shutdown over, funding for the SNAP program is flowing again, and the Department of Transportation is easing flight cuts at 40 major airports due to improved air traffic controller staffing. However, it may take weeks for the Low Income Home Energy Assistance Program (HEAP) to reopen. On Capitol Hill, some moderate House Republicans are pushing to address expiring Affordable Care Act subsidies.

A Republican effort to redraw Indiana's congressional map to shore up the party's House majority appears to be dead after the GOP leader of the state Senate announced the chamber will not convene in December for that purpose.

New York State and New York City News and Politics

In New York City, the covert race for City Council speaker is taking shape, with the 17-member Brooklyn delegation and its leader, Rodneyse Bichotte Hermelyn, aiming to act as a unified voting bloc. According to City & State, front-runners for the position include Julie Menin and Crystal Hudson, with Amanda Farías seen as a potential compromise candidate.

The Trump administration is exploring the use of a Coast Guard facility on Staten Island to detain immigrants, which would expand federal detention capacity in the city. The move coincides with a surge in the federal government's use of New York county jails for U.S. Immigration and Customs Enforcement detentions this year. Meanwhile, a federal judge has thrown out a Trump administration lawsuit against the city of Rochester over its sanctuary city policy.

A judge has ordered a temporary halt to a new agreement between New York City and Mets owner Steve Cohen regarding the use of the Citi Field parking lot. The ruling could potentially torpedo Cohen’s bid to bring a casino to the Queens site.

Administrators at the Brooklyn Hospital Center are sounding the alarm that the hospital could be forced to shut down by the end of the year if it does not receive financial aid from Gov. Kathy Hochul's administration.

Conservationists are renewing pressure on Gov. Hochul to approve a ban on the harvesting of horseshoe crabs in state waters. The governor vetoed a similar measure last year.

International News and Politics

The Trump administration’s dismantling of the U.S. Agency for International Development (USAID) and halt to global assistance has had devastating consequences, according to Dr. Atul Gawande in an interview with The Bulwark. Gawande, a former Biden administration official, cited an estimate that the policy has already led to 600,000 deaths from disease and starvation, 400,000 of them children. A new documentary, "Rovina’s Choice," follows a Sudanese refugee in Kenya dealing with her daughter's severe malnutrition, highlighting the human toll of the cuts.

President Trump told reporters he has "sort of" decided how to move forward on Venezuela following several high-level meetings on potential military operations in the country. A classified Department of Justice brief justifying recent U.S. boat strikes in the Caribbean reportedly describes fentanyl as a potential chemical weapons threat.

The Trump Organization is reportedly in talks for a branded property within a major Saudi Arabian government-owned real estate development. The project is overseen by Crown Prince Mohammed bin Salman, who is scheduled to visit Washington next week.

Russia continues its efforts to capture the eastern Ukrainian city of Pokrovsk. In recent days, Russian forces have also pummeled Kyiv with drones and missiles, killing at least six people and targeting the country’s power grid as winter approaches.

U.S. envoy Steve Witkoff is planning to meet with chief Hamas negotiator Khalil al-Hayya as the administration continues to monitor the fragile peace transition. In Gaza, many residents remain displaced without running water or electricity.

The BBC is facing a major crisis after editing a speech by President Trump. Following an apology from the broadcaster, Trump announced he plans to sue for as much as $5 billion.

Culture and Technology

A growing number of Americans, particularly those under 30, are turning to A.I. chatbots like ChatGPT for medical advice. According to The New York Times, users are drawn to the technology's accessibility and empathetic tone, but medical professionals express concern about the potential for chatbots to provide incomplete or fabricated answers for high-stakes health decisions.

In a significant genetic breakthrough, scientists have sequenced RNA from the remains of a 39,000-year-old woolly mammoth named Yuka. The research, published in the journal Cell, pushes back the record for the oldest RNA sequenced by 25,000 years. The analysis also revealed that Yuka, long thought to be female, was in fact male.

Cable news channel MSNBC officially rebranded as MS NOW this morning. The change comes as the channel has seen a 34 percent decline in its total audience this year, reflecting a splintering media ecosystem where podcasts and newsletters increasingly compete for attention.

Director Guillermo del Toro's long-awaited adaptation of Mary Shelley's "Frankenstein" has been released on Netflix. Meanwhile, Hollywood's box office is in a slump; The New York Times reports that not one of the 25 dramas and comedies released in North American theaters over the past three months has become a hit.

Journalist Olivia Nuzzi has released a new book detailing her digital relationship with Robert F. Kennedy Jr., a scandal that upended her career last year.

David Szalay's sixth novel, "Flesh," won the prestigious Booker Prize last week. Folk musician and humorist Todd Snider has died at the age of 59.
